About Us

Who We Are

Wittmer Associates / George B. Wittmer Associates, Inc was incorporated in the Fall of 1994 as a Florida-registered environmental waste services and management company, originally focused upon petroleum contaminated soil management and spill-site remediation. In 1998, Wittmer acquired BMH Materials, a drummed and bulk waste processing facility in Green Cove Springs, FL, and in the same year, we entered the pulp and paper industry as a contracted bulk waste processor and beneficial re-use provider for (the former) Jefferson Smurfit Corporation.

And over the past fifteen years, Wittmer continually expanded into long-term pulp and paper relationships to provide heavy equipment and experienced personnel for large volume residual recovery, beneficial re-use, site services, and landfill management operations for clients such as Boise Cascade, Smurfit Stone Container, RockTenn, Packaging Corporation of America, and Georgia Pacific -- at pulp and paper mills in Florida, Georgia, and Alabama.

Our beneficial re-use services are supported by responsible relationships with environmental regulatory agencies and scientists of CH2MHill and all of the processed by-products Wittmer handles for our upstream clients carry registered product labels issued by various states' Departments of Agriculture.

What We Want

Live on

Green Planet
GBWA, Inc. annually recycles hundreds of thousands of cubic yards of various industrial discard streams and supplies millions of pounds of soil amendments, soil blends, and potting soil materials. Every day, we recover, recondition, process, and ship (by truck, rail, and barge) a very wide assortment of recovered residual streams in large volumes such as papermill organic sludge, fly ash, precipitated carbonates, flume grit, wood yard waste, off-specification boiler fuel solids, stockpiled bark, shives, knots, butts, and other materials previously landfilled. Wittmer eliminates waste stream management, handling, storage and liability by efficiently transforming industrial residual streams into commodities for use by other industries.
